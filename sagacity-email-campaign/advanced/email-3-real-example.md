I want to share a story about someone in a position similar to yours.

A services business owner came to us frustrated with proposals. Every new opportunity meant three hours of pulling together case studies, tailoring the scope, writing the narrative, and formatting the document. The work was good, but the process was draining and it meant saying no to smaller opportunities that could not justify the time.

We built a custom AI workflow that drew from their existing project data, matched relevant case studies automatically, and generated a first draft of each proposal section based on the brief. The owner still reviewed and refined every proposal, but the starting point was 80 percent there instead of a blank page. The whole process went from three hours to twenty minutes.

What changed was not just the time saved. They started responding to opportunities they would have previously ignored. Within three months, two of those "would have skipped it" proposals turned into their most profitable projects that quarter.

This is what compounding looks like in practice. One workflow change opens doors that were not visible before.
